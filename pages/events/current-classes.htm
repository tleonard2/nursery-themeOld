September 14 - 15, 2019

Gardening with Native Plants

with Linda Hardison and Dennis Albert of OregonFlora
Learn about native plants, what constitutes a “native,” and which species will grow well in your Willamette Valley garden or landscape. Learn how the design of your native plant garden can be informed by the resources of OregonFlora. OregonFlora, a program based at Oregon State University, provides comprehensive information about the native and naturalized plants of our state. Through the book, Flora of Oregon, the wildflower identification app, Facebook page, and the OregonFlora website, users can find photos, maps, and details about Oregon native plants suited to gardens, and where these can be purchased.

How to make Kokedama (moss ball) using PNW Native Plants

with Weina Dinata of Poppy and Finch
Kokedama, which means literally moss ball, is a Japanese traditional bonsai form that is also called Poor Man's Bonsai. Join Weina, from Poppy & Finch, to watch and learn how to make a moss ball using our PNW native plants. She'll start the demonstration by sharing her passion and knowledge on how important native plants' role to our habitat followed by showing how versatile they really are to be used in different form or style of planting. You'll come home with a the step-by-step, care instructions and resources hand-out.

The basics of Begonia Propagation

Raquel Branscum and Deann Orr of the Pacific Northwest Branch of the American Begonia Society
Raquel Branscum and Deann Orr will demonstrate easy ways to multiply your begonias using stem cuttings, leaf cuttings, rhizome cuttings and by seed sowing. Begonias are one of the most rewarding plants to propagate, and following the examples in this interesting demonstration will yield baby begonia plants by the dozens. Many of these methods can be used for other houseplants too! This talk is part of The Fall Exotic, Rare & Unusual Tropical Houseplant Sale at Portland Nursery brought to us by the Mt. Hood Gesneriad Society & the Pacific Northwest Begonia Society.

Firescaping

with Bonnie Bruce of Celilo Gardens

An Introduction to Gesneriads: How to Grow Them Indoors and Under Lights

with Ann Amato from Cistus Nursery
Come join us as the Mt. Hood Gesneriad Society introduces us to the plant family that includes classic houseplant gems such as Streptocarpus, Primulina, Petrocosmea, Gloxinia, Sinningia, African violets, and so many more! Learn about their care and cultivation and find out about some of their more unusual family members. Bring your questions too! Plants will be ranked in terms of their ease of care and tips will be included on how to better grow the more fussy ones. Included will also be a basic introduction to growing plants under lights.

This talk is part of The Fall Exotic, Rare & Unusual Tropical Houseplant Sale at Portland Nursery brought to us by the Mt. Hood Gesneriad Society & the Pacific Northwest Begonia Society.

Repotting your Orchid

with Sue Volek of the Oregon Orchid Society
Orchid lovers can bring one or two of their personal plants and learn to repot them in this hands-on class. Sue Volek of the Oregon Orchid Society has repotted literally hundreds and hundreds of these beauties over the past 25 years, and she'll explain why, when and how to repot keeping the roots alive and the plants happy. Orchid mix (both bark and coconut husk mixed with perlite and horticultural charcoal) will be provided, along with scissors, gloves, plastic pots, and tags. NOTE: please make sure your plants are bug-free, and bring them in a sealed plastic bag, just in case.
