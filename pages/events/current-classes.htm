Intro to Bonsai - Gardening with Living Art

with Keith Wingfield, Bonsai Society Of Portland
Keith Wingfield, Chairman of the BSOP Ambassador Program, will be assisted by several volunteer Ambassador trainees in presenting a brief discussion of the history and origins of the art of Bonsai. Participants will be able to observe Bonsai trees from his and the trainees’ personal collections, ranging in size from very large to palm-size, and ranging in age from a few years to decades. The processes necessary to maintain the health and development of these pieces of living art over decades will be explained. Bonsai is a year-round gardening pursuit, providing engagement in multiple horticultural practices in every season. BSOP invites you to join the joy and discovery of Bonsai! Come early to enjoy a stroll through the annual BSOP Bonsai exhibit!

Growing Nutrition Packed Vegetables

with Marc Scheidecker
Is there really a difference between the average vegetable plant and one grown for maximum levels of nutrition? A study done by Rutgers University researchers reported mineral variations as great as 1,938 parts per million of iron in some tomatoes as opposed to only one ppm in other tomatoes of the same variety! There is a lot we can do to raise the level of nutrients in our vegetables. Healthy plants can only come from healthy soil. Come learn how to grow more nutritious vegetables by improving your soil.

Milkweeds and Monarchs in the Pacific Northwest: A Growing Passion

with Elaine Kelley of Milkweed & Mustard Seed
The endangered Monarch butterfly is rarely seen in our little corner of northwestern Oregon. Milkweed, its host plant, is just as scarce. Learn what entomologists and other scientists are saying now about the changing trajectory of the great Monarch migration and the importance of expanding the presence of milkweed in our area. Join the movement to save this iconic butterfly from extinction!

The Versatile Begonia

with Stephanie Willis, member, Pacific Northwest Branch of the American Begonia Society; and member, the American Begonia Society. 
Summer to Fall is the “show time” for many types of begonias, from the stunning tuberous beauties, to the stately angel wing or cane types that have large clusters of flowers, to the wax begonias that are a durable mainstay of semi-shade plantings. Add in the exotic rex types with multi-colored leaves, and the rhizomatous with unusual leaf shapes, textures and colors and you have a family of plants to fill most gardeners warm weather landscape dreams. And they are easy to grow!

Stephanie Willis, a long time member of the American Begonia society, will give care tips for each of these types of begonias and show examples of rare terrarium types that can extend your begonia growing interest year round!

5050 SE STARK • Saturday • July 20 • 1:00pm
