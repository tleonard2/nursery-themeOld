Seed Saving at Home

with Katie Frey of Portland Nursery
You Too Can Be A Seed Saver! Please join us as we dive into the ins and outs of seed-saving. In this hands-on class, we’ll learn how to tell seed appearance, ripeness, viability, collection, and storage needs. This class is primarily on ornamental/native species, but we will also touch on vegetable seed basics. Be prepared to leave with a new passion for the fun adventure of DIY seeds!

Planting Garlic in your Home Vegetable Garden

with Ian Wilson of Portland Edible Gardens
Planting garlic cloves in mid-October is the final act of planting in the vegetable garden for the year.  What's more, garlic is one of the easiest vegetables to grow in your garden. Come learn about garlic types and varieties, proper spacing and planting, where to buy 'seed garlic' and how to care for your garlic for a bountiful harvest!

Make Your Own Clay Leaf Bowl!

with interdisciplinary artist Tamara Lynne, of Portland Nursery
Making a decorative leaf bowl is a simple and beautiful way to commemorate fall! Using fresh autumn leaves to make prints in the clay, each participant will create their own unique work of art, which they can paint or glaze after it is dry. Air-dry clay is perfect for making decorative seasonal objects that will not get wet. This leaf bowl will be appropriate for holding coins or small items, but not for food. Feel free to bring fresh leaves that you would like to imprint into your design!

Class limited to 12 participants. Materials fee payable at register: $10.

Aromatic Herbs for Winter Wellness

with Missy Rohs, Arctos School of Herbal Studies
Join Missy Rohs of the Arctos School of Herbal & Botanical Studies to learn about the array of aromatic medicinal plants that can be used to support winter wellness. These healing herbs — many of which are found in your garden or your kitchen — can support the immune system, promote respiratory health, and even alleviate seasonal affective symptoms. We’ll talk about how to use these plants safely and effectively.

Top Ten Orchid Myths

with Sue Volek of the Oregon Orchid Society
Sue will explain why the top 10 orchid myths are just that: myths. She'll talk about how to grow these lovely plants, including light, water and temperature requirements; how and when to repot them; and explain how the most common varieties are easier to grow than you think. Sue's been growing orchids for 25 years, and since she's made every mistake in the book you won't have to.

5050 SE STARK • Saturday • October 26 • 11:00am
Click here to register for this class.

Repotting your Orchid

with Sue Volek of the Oregon Orchid Society
Orchid lovers can bring one or two of their personal plants and learn to repot them in this hands-on class. Sue Volek of the Oregon Orchid Society has repotted literally hundreds and hundreds of these beauties over the past 25 years, and she'll explain why, when and how to repot keeping the roots alive and the plants happy. Orchid mix (both bark and coconut husk mixed with perlite and horticultural charcoal) will be provided, along with scissors, gloves, plastic pots, and tags. NOTE: please make sure your plants are bug-free, and bring them in a sealed plastic bag, just in case.

Class fee: $15.00 Class Limit: 15.

Firescaping

with Bonnie Bruce of Celilo Gardens
In the last few years it seems summers are getting hotter and fire “season” stretches out for longer.  And living in an urban environment is not always protection against a wildfire. Whether you have a vacation home secluded in a quiet spot in the forest or live at the edge of an urban wildlife refuge it might be wise to consider strategies for Firescaping your landscape.  Bonnie Bruce will discuss designing landscapes to resist fires, including plants that are not easily combustible, plants that are combustible, and management strategies which homeowners can implement.
