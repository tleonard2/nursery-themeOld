Winter Windfall Colors
dyeing with storm fallen branches, orchard prunings and lichens.

with Iris Sullivan-Daire of DreamBird Studio.
Sweetly scented healing colors on silk and wool ~ warm browns & rich burnished golden hues, these are the reward for the forager of winter windfall dyes here in the PNW. We will discuss the ethics and safety of wild crafting dyes, possibilities for using fruit tree prunings for color, and touch on the medicinal potential of some common trees and lichens.

Students will leave with a cozy raw silk scarf dyed in the class, and empowered to explore the potential of windfall colors in their own neighborhood.

with Linda Hardison and Dennis Albert of OregonFlora
Learn about native plants, what constitutes a 'native', the ecoregions of our state, and which species will grow well in your Willamette Valley garden or landscape. Learn how the design of your native plant gardening choices can be informed by the resources of OregonFlora through discussion and lots of beautiful images. OregonFlora is a program based at Oregon State University that provides comprehensive information about the native and naturalized plants of our state that grow in in the wild.
