Kokedama Workshop

with Lucy Davenport of Bonsai Akira
Kokedama roughly translates from Japanese to mean “moss ball” – describing the wrapping of the plant’s root’s and soil in moss. It’s a beautiful and space-saving way to display houseplants, once artistically wrapped with string and hung from a ceiling or shelf, or set on a tray. Learn how to create one in this hands-on workshop and go home with tips on how to care for your new creation.

$30 fee, payable at register day of class. Class size is limited to 15 participants.

Fresh Indigo Silk Ribbon

with Iris Sullivan of Dreambird Studio
Japanese Indigo, Persicaria tinctoria, is an easy to grow annual here in the Northwest, with an abundance of lush leaves and lovely flowers. It is also one of the plants that contains the rare and beautiful blue pigment known as indigo. Come learn how to use the fresh leaves to dye silk a gorgeous turquoise blue, a color only possible when you grow your own. In this very hands on workshop participants will dye silk ribbon during class, and receive an indigo plant to take home for future dyeing and seed saving.

Advance registration required, class size limited to 25. Materials fee $30.

Indigo Dyed Gift Cloth

with Iris Sullivan of Dreambird Studio
Indigo is known as the king of dyes. True Blue pigment is rare in the natural world, and plant sources for it have been revered in many cultures. The ancient art of dyeing with natural indigo in a vat is filled with magic and delight. For this workshop participants will learn how pigment extracted from home grown Japanese indigo can be used to create true blue on natural fibers. Participants will dye a small cotton gift cloth to take home during this hands-on class.

Pre-registration required, class size limited to 15. Materials fee is $30.

Gardening with Native Plants

with Linda Hardison and Dennis Albert of OregonFlora
Learn about native plants, what constitutes a “native,” and which species will grow well in your Willamette Valley garden or landscape. Learn how the design of your native plant garden can be informed by the resources of OregonFlora. OregonFlora, a program based at Oregon State University, provides comprehensive information about the native and naturalized plants of our state. Through the book, Flora of Oregon, the wildflower identification app, Facebook page, and the OregonFlora website, users can find photos, maps, and details about Oregon native plants suited to gardens, and where these can be purchased.
