with Brittany Hobbs

Let's give our plants a helping hand by imitating their growing environment and making them moss poles!! The Versatile Begonia

with Stephanie Willis, member, Pacific Northwest Branch of the American Begonia Society; and member, the American Begonia Society. 
Summer to Fall is the “show time” for many types of begonias, from the stunning tuberous beauties, to the stately angel wing or cane types that have large clusters of flowers, to the wax begonias that are a durable mainstay of semi-shade plantings. Add in the exotic rex types with multi-colored leaves, and the rhizomatous with unusual leaf shapes, textures and colors and you have a family of plants to fill most gardeners warm weather landscape dreams. And they are easy to grow!

Stephanie Willis, a long time member of the American Begonia society, will give care tips for each of these types of begonias and show examples of rare terrarium types that can extend your begonia growing interest year round!

Intro to Permaculture Principles

with Shelby Spencer of Veteran's Ecological Trades Collective
This class focuses on making permaculture (sustainable agriculture that mimics natural ecosystems) knowledge accessible, easy, and fun! Permaculture is a creative design process based on whole-systems thinking that seeks to emulate the complex balance of natural systems.

Using these patterns and principles we can find in nature can be used in all aspects of life, from agriculture to natural building, from biomimicry technology to education and even economics. Whether you want start planning your beehives to or increase the yield of your organic garden, this class is for you. Together we will explore principles of permaculture for application in rural and urban areas, -especially for your own back yard! Please bring writing materials, and plenty of enthusiasm!

Kokedama Workshop

with Lucy Davenport of Bonsai Akira
Kokedama roughly translates from Japanese to mean “moss ball” – describing the wrapping of the plant’s root’s and soil in moss. It’s a beautiful and space-saving way to display houseplants, once artistically wrapped with string and hung from a ceiling or shelf, or set on a tray. Learn how to create one in this hands-on workshop and go home with tips on how to care for your new creation.

Fresh Indigo Silk Ribbon

with Iris Sullivan of Dreambird Studio
Japanese Indigo, Persicaria tinctoria, is an easy to grow annual here in the Northwest, with an abundance of lush leaves and lovely flowers. It is also one of the plants that contains the rare and beautiful blue pigment known as indigo. Come learn how to use the fresh leaves to dye silk a gorgeous turquoise blue, a color only possible when you grow your own. In this very hands on workshop participants will dye silk ribbon during class, and receive an indigo plant to take home for future dyeing and seed saving.

Advance registration required, class size limited to 25. Materials fee $30.

Indigo Dyed Gift Cloth

with Iris Sullivan of Dreambird Studio
Indigo is known as the king of dyes. True Blue pigment is rare in the natural world, and plant sources for it have been revered in many cultures. The ancient art of dyeing with natural indigo in a vat is filled with magic and delight. For this workshop participants will learn how pigment extracted from home grown Japanese indigo can be used to create true blue on natural fibers. Participants will dye a small cotton gift cloth to take home during this hands-on class.
