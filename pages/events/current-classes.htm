Intro to Bonsai - Gardening with Living Art

with Keith Wingfield, Bonsai Society Of Portland
Keith Wingfield, Chairman of the BSOP Ambassador Program, will be assisted by several volunteer Ambassador trainees in presenting a brief discussion of the history and origins of the art of Bonsai. Participants will be able to observe Bonsai trees from his and the trainees’ personal collections, ranging in size from very large to palm-size, and ranging in age from a few years to decades. The processes necessary to maintain the health and development of these pieces of living art over decades will be explained. Bonsai is a year-round gardening pursuit, providing engagement in multiple horticultural practices in every season. BSOP invites you to join the joy and discovery of Bonsai! Milkweeds and Monarchs in the Pacific Northwest: A Growing Passion

with Elaine Kelley of Milkweed & Mustard Seed
The endangered Monarch butterfly is rarely seen in our little corner of northwestern Oregon. Milkweed, its host plant, is just as scarce. Learn what entomologists and other scientists are saying now about the changing trajectory of the great Monarch migration and the importance of expanding the presence of milkweed in our area. Join the movement to save this iconic butterfly from extinction!
