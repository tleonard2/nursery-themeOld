Repotting your Orchid

with Sue Volek of the Oregon Orchid Society
Orchid lovers can bring one or two of their personal plants and learn to repot them in this hands-on class. Sue Volek of the Oregon Orchid Society has repotted literally hundreds and hundreds of these beauties over the past 25 years, and she'll explain why, when and how to repot keeping the roots alive and the plants happy. Orchid mix (both bark and coconut husk mixed with perlite and horticultural charcoal) will be provided, along with scissors, gloves, plastic pots, and tags. NOTE: please make sure your plants are bug-free, and bring them in a sealed plastic bag, just in case.

Introduction to Rain Gardens

with Whitney Bailey, Senior Conservationist at East Multnomah Soil and Water Conservation District
Come learn about rain gardens! In this presentation you’ll learn what stormwater is and how we can better manage it to protect the environment. You will come away understanding how to: assess whether your site is suitable for a rain garden, determine the best location for it, calculate impervious surface and the size of your rain garden, choose appropriate plants, and maintain your garden for years of enjoyment. You will also be provided with information and resources for further learning.

Firescaping

with Bonnie Bruce of Celilo Gardens
In the last few years it seems summers are getting hotter and fire “season” stretches out for longer.  And living in an urban environment is not always protection against a wildfire. Whether you have a vacation home secluded in a quiet spot in the forest or live at the edge of an urban wildlife refuge it might be wise to consider strategies for Firescaping your landscape.  Bonnie Bruce will discuss designing landscapes to resist fires, including plants that are not easily combustible, plants that are combustible, and management strategies which homeowners can implement.

Captured Sunlight: Dyeing with Marigolds

with Iris Sullivan Daire
Marigolds, the familiar flower beloved by gardeners, are used to adorn altars in many cultures. They also happen to make a delightful dye plant, with capacity to dye silk nearly the same happy color as the flowers themselves! In this fun, hands-on class, students will learn the basics of dyeing with flowers, and come away with a long silk scarf they dye themselves. As we enter the season of darkness, silk can be used to adorn your home or yourself with the colors of the sun. Students will receive a packet of seeds for "Shades of Gold" Marigold so they can look forward to making flower garlands and using their new knowledge for more dyeing sessions next summer. Materials fee: $30 payable to instructor.

Pre-registration is Required!
