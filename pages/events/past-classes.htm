Unusual Begonias and Gesneriads for Indoor Growing

with Stephanie Willis of the Mt. Hood Gesneriad and the American Begonia Society
Stephanie Willis will tell us about the different kinds of begonias and gesneriads that make interesting indoor winter gardening. From miniature relatives of florist’s gloxinias to spectacular flowering plants to some real oddities, the gesneriad family’s variety goes far beyond its most famous member, the African violet. In addition, begonias are not just the ubiquitous summer bloomers, but include many smaller types whose leaves are jaw-droppingly beautiful. Stephanie will show examples from her collection, including some new species from Asia, and give growing tips for these beauties.

Kokedama Workshop

with Lucy Davenport of Bonsai Akira
Kokedama roughly translates from Japanese to mean “moss ball,” which describes the technique of wrapping of the plant’s roots and soil in moss and artistically tying this bundle with wire or string. Either hung from a ceiling or shelf or set on a tray, kokedama is a beautiful and space saving way to display houseplants. Learn how to create two in this class and go home with tips on how to care for your new creations. $30 fee, payable at register day of class.

Top 10 Orchid Myths

with Sue Volek of the Oregon Orchid Society
There's lots of tips out there about growing orchids. Some are true and some are false. This talk will debunk some of the misperceptions about these beautiful plants and give you the bottom line on how to select, grow, and bloom the easiest orchid plants. Sue Volek has been growing orchids for almost 20 years. A journalist and communications person during the day, she would come home and tend her plants at night until they took over not only the kitchen, but the living room, dining room, and encroached on the rest of the house. Her current favorites are slipper orchids, but since she never met an orchid she didn't like, Sue has an array of plants from Ascocendas to Zygopetalums. She is president of the Oregon Orchid Society.

Moss Pole Plant Stake Tutorial

with Tiffany Garner
Large vining houseplants like monsteras and philodendrons are natural climbers and prefer to grow on other plants in their tropical habitats. Providing a good support system for them in your home will reward you with larger leaves and a healthier plant. In this hour long class you will learn how to make two different types of plant poles from sphagnum moss and coconut coir. With this knowledge you'll be able to make plant poles of all sizes for your specific plants!

Gardening with Native Plants

with Linda Hardison and Dennis Albert of OregonFlora
Learn about native plants, what constitutes a 'native', and which species will grow well in your Willamette Valley garden or landscape. Learn how the design of your native plant gardening choices can be informed by the resources of OregonFlora through discussion and lots of beautiful images. OregonFlora is a program based at Oregon State University that provides comprehensive information about the native and naturalized plants of our state that grow in in the wild. Through its Flora book, wildflower identification app, Facebook page, and the oregonflora.org website, users can find photos, maps, and details about Oregon's plants, including garden species and where to purchase them.
