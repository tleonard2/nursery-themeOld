Chock full of color choices in cheery trailers and charming uprights! Fuchsias offer an amazing flower show June through frost.

Most tender fuchsia grow in sun provided there is some shade in the hottest part of the day. Some varieties, such as Henna, have totally different coloring in sun versus shade

Tender Fuchsia are:

  • Hummingbird magnets
  • Wonderful in mixed pots. Upright varieties are thrillers, compact or lax upright varieties for fillers and trailing varieties for spillers
  • Variety of flower forms- fluffy doubles, elegant singles and sweet minis
  • Fuchsia fruits are edible (and make your tongue tingle)
  • Flowers even in full shade!
  • Plant with Quench and Osmocote to make watering and fertilizing easier.

Fuchsia FAQs

Do I need to fertilize?

Yes. Fuchsias will stay full and blooming if you fertilize at least twice a month with a liquid fertilizer.

Or add a starter fertilizer and Osmocote to the soil when you plant.

How do I save my fuchsia for next year?

The Oregon Fuchsia society has great instructions for this!
